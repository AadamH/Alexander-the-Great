King philip of Macedonia

In Ancient Greece there were two powerful cities economically politically and military. Athens a city where Democracy was born. And Sparta known for its militaristic way of life. Sparta and Athens got in a war called the peloponnesian war Athens as the Delian league and sparta as the peloponnesian league. Both sides took no notice of a city called Macedonia that would become a military power.

Up North of Greece there was a city called Macedonia where its king Philip decided to build Macedonian armies. He created many reforms to the military. He passed a law making military service full time and he created a new spear called the Sarissa an 18 inch spear longer than the ordinary spear. The phalanx was crucial as it was effective against cavlary and phalanx attacks but was vulnerable to artillery fire such as arrows

He created the macedonian phalanx with soldiers sixteen ranks deep trained with the Sarissa to lead the formation. making it a difficult formation to penetrate.

After spending many years building his armies he decided to take Greek territories. He was successful in defeating Athenian and Thebian armies at the battle of Chaeronea. Through alliances and conquests he formed a confederation called the League of Corinth, with him as the elected hegemon and commander-in-chief of Greece. Only Sparta refused to join his alliance.

He decided to invade the Persian empire but was assassinated by his bodyguard Pausanias of Orestis, led to the immediate succession of his son Alexander, who was already an experienced commander would go on to invade the Achaemenid Empire in his father's stead.

Greek invasion of the Persian Empire

Alexander was now the king of Macedonia he decided to invade the Persian empire after securing his position in greece executing potential rivals and putting down rebellion. Once his position in Greece was secured he crossed the Hellespont into Asia and won the battle of the Granicus River.

After winning the battle of the Granicus alexander quickly expanded in the Persian empire and took the cities of Miletus, Halicarnassus, Sardis Gordium.

Alexander received reports of the main Persian army so he advanced to the Nur mountains near the syrian border to find it. But the Persian army flanked them and trapped them in the mountains. The army was Led by King Darius III who had 10,000 soldiers in his army even his bodyguards the best Persian soldier called the Immortals.

Despite outnumbered Alexander won a decisive victory. Darius retreated further in his empire alexander resumed his advance.

He advanced to Egypt Africa where the Persians surrendered the Royal treasury. Egyptians welcomed him and saw him as their liberator from the Persians and crowned him pharoah. He founded a city in the Nile which he named after himself Alexandria.

Even they did not stand a chance against the Macedonians the Spartans were massacred Agis was killed by a Macedonian spear man forcing the Spartans to sue for Peace.

Battle of Guagamela

Alexander received an offer from Darius asking for peace offering him gold, his daughter hand in marriage and half of the Persian empire, Alexander refused.

He found Darius`s army at Guagamela where every soldier from the Persian empire was in his army even war elephants but he won a victory. This battle led to the end of the Persian empire. Darius was assassinated by Bessus in the city of Ecbatana and called himself King of Persia.

Alexander ordered Darius to be buried and wanted to find and kill Bessus. When he reached Kunduz Bessus was brought to him in chains he ordered Bessus execution.

Alexander`s Death and accomplishments

With Asia and some of Africa in his control Alexander decided to invade Arabia (Saudi Arabia) today he quickly collapsed and died at aged 31. No one knows the reason of his death historians beleieve he had Malaria which was very common in that era.

Before his death Alexander did not leave any plans for a successor so his generals fought amongst each other in a civil war carving out empires of their own. In Africa the civil war led to the rise of the Ptolemaic empire in Egypt.
